Patient: Jane Smith
Date: 2024-08-12
Age: 29
Chief Complaint: Persistent cough.

Subjective:
The patient is a 29-year-old female with no significant past medical history. She presents with a 2-week history of persistent dry cough. The patient reports that the cough is more pronounced at night and is sometimes accompanied by a sore throat. She denies fever, chills, chest pain, or shortness of breath. The patient works as a school teacher and reports that several of her students have had similar symptoms recently.

Objective:

Vital Signs: BP 120/80 mmHg, HR 70 bpm, RR 18/min, SpO2 99% on room air.
Physical Exam:
General: The patient is well-appearing and in no acute distress.
ENT: Throat is slightly erythematous without exudates. No cervical lymphadenopathy.
Respiratory: Lungs are clear to auscultation with no wheezing, rales, or rhonchi.
Cardiovascular: Regular rate and rhythm, no murmurs, rubs, or gallops.
Abdomen: Soft, non-tender, non-distended.
Assessment:

Likely viral upper respiratory infection.
Cough secondary to postnasal drip.
Plan:

Recommend supportive care including increased fluid intake, rest, and throat lozenges.
Prescribe a short course of over-the-counter cough suppressants.
Advise the patient to return if symptoms persist for more than 2 weeks or worsen.
No need for antibiotics at this time as symptoms are consistent with a viral infection.
